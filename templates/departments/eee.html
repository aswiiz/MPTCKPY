Overview

Driving the Energy Revolution

The Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering (EEE) is dedicated to teaching the generation, distribution, and utilization of electrical energy alongside electronic control systems.

Electrical engineering is one of the oldest, broadest, and most vital branches of engineering. From the micro-scale of electronic chips to the macro-scale of national power grids, EEE encompasses it all. Our program integrates traditional power systems with cutting-edge renewable energy technologies and smart grid architectures. Students are trained to solve complex problems related to power transmission, industrial automation, and energy efficiency, preparing them for a world rapidly transitioning to sustainable energy.

Why Choose EEE?

  • Evergreen Industry

    As long as humanity uses electricity, Electrical Engineers will be in incredibly high demand worldwide.

  • Core of the Green Transition

    Be at the forefront of the fight against climate change by designing renewable energy systems and EVs.

  • Versatile Skillset

    Gain expertise in heavy machinery as well as delicate microelectronics, doubling your career options.

Major Subjects Covered

Our curriculum provides a comprehensive understanding of both heavy electrical systems and delicate control electronics.

Electrical Machines

Design, operation, and control of transformers, AC/DC motors, and generators.

Power Systems

Generation, transmission, and distribution of electrical power across national grids.

Industrial Automation

Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs), SCADA systems, and robotic assembly lines.

Renewable Energy

Solar PV systems, wind turbines, hydro-power, and sustainable energy integration.

Electrical Installation

Domestic and industrial wiring, safety regulations, grounding, and cost estimation.

Smart Grid Technologies

Modernizing power grids with digital communication, sensors, and automated controls.

Skills You Will Gain

  • Technical Skills: Machine winding, power flow analysis, switchgear maintenance, and high-voltage testing.
  • Software Tools: AutoCAD Electrical, ETAP, MATLAB Simulink, and PLC programming suites.
  • Industry Skills: Energy auditing, electrical safety compliance (NEC), and industrial project management.

High-Voltage Laboratories

Where theoretical physics meets industrial reality

Electrical Machines Lab

Equipped with heavy-duty induction motors, synchronous generators, and large-scale transformers for load testing and characteristics analysis.

Power Systems Lab

Features grid simulation panels, transmission line models, and switchgear protection relays to study real-world grid behavior.

Renewable Energy & Automation Lab

A modern workspace with solar PV training modules, wind energy simulators, and industrial PLC control stations.
