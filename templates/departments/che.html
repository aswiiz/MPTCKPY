Computer Hardware Engineering

Building the Foundations of Technology

Overview

Engineering Robust Systems

The Department of Computer Hardware Engineering offers a specialized curriculum that focuses on the physical components of computing systems, networking hardware, and the integration of hardware and software.

As the world increasingly relies on cloud computing, IoT devices, and complex server infrastructures, the need for skilled hardware engineers has never been higher. Our program is designed to provide hands-on experience in assembling, troubleshooting, and maintaining computer architectures. We bridge the critical gap between electronic circuitry and software execution, ensuring our students understand how processors, memory, and peripherals communicate at the lowest levels.

Why Choose Hardware Engineering?

  • Essential Industry Backbone

    Every software application relies on hardware to run. You will be the architect of the physical systems that power the digital world.

  • Growth of IoT and Edge Computing

    The explosion of smart devices requires hardware experts to design and maintain interconnected physical systems.

  • Hands-on Problem Solving

    Perfect for students who enjoy tactile learning, building physical machines, and troubleshooting complex technical issues.

Major Subjects Covered

Our curriculum provides a deep dive into the physical architecture of modern computing.

Computer Architecture

Microprocessors, memory hierarchy, and instruction set architectures.

Hardware Maintenance

Diagnostic testing, motherboard repair, and peripheral troubleshooting.

Networking Hardware

Physical configuration of routers, switches, cabling, and network topologies.

Embedded Systems

Microcontrollers (Arduino, Raspberry Pi), sensor integration, and real-time processing.

IoT Devices

Building connected devices, wireless communication, and smart home technologies.

System & Server Admin

Linux server configuration, virtualization, and enterprise hardware management.

Skills You Will Gain

  • Technical Skills: Hardware assembly, soldering, circuit testing, and network configuration.
  • Software Tools: Wireshark, Cisco Packet Tracer, OS deployment tools, and diagnostics software.
  • Industry Skills: IT infrastructure planning, hardware lifecycle management, and disaster recovery.

Specialized Laboratories

Where theory meets physical execution

Hardware Assembly Lab

Equipped with hundreds of components for students to practice building, disassembling, and troubleshooting PCs and servers.

Networking Infrastructure Lab

Features enterprise-grade racks, patch panels, and switching equipment for physical network design and maintenance.

Embedded Systems & IoT Lab

A creative space filled with microcontrollers, sensors, and actuators for building smart devices and automation systems.
